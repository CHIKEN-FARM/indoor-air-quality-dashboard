Vacuum Cleaner Selection

Gold Standard (Most Expensive)

Externally Vented Central Vacuum


Air Quality Advantages :
  • External venting means all particles not trapped by the bag are blown outside the house
  • Powerful suction means more stray particles are captured during vacuuming
  • System is sealed which means after vacuuming the outlets are closed and no smells can enter the house from previously vacuumed dust
Other Advantages :
  • Bags only need to be emptied once or twice per year
  • Less weight to lug around
  • Quieter when vacuuming
  • Simple canister design means one rubber seal to replace if it fails
  • Save money on HEPA filters
  • Large selection of standard fitting attachments, brushes, crevice tools
Note: HEPA filtration is not needed with an external vent and only adds maintenance costs
Bags are recommended for Asthmatics because dust is contained when throwing out full bags

A similar level of cleaning can be achieved with a central vacuum located outside the living envelope (such as in a garage).

American Lung Association recommends an Externally Vented Central Vacuum

Silver Standard (Cheapest)



Long Hoses

Externally Vented Shop Vacuum

This could be the cheapest solution. Use a shop vacuum with an external vent port (also known as a blower). Connect the vent port to an appropriate flexible tube, and when vacuuming place the tube outside the window.
Ensure that the vacuum is properly sealed - check for any air escaping by feeling around the vacuum canister and hoses while it is running to be sure there are no air leaks inside the home.
Keep the window as closed as possible only allowing space for the tube.

Air Quality Advantages :
  • External venting means all particles not trapped by the bag are blown outside the house
Other Advantages :
  • Simple canister design means one rubber seal to replace if it fails
  • Save money on HEPA filters

Bronze Standard (Medium price)



Nilfisk GD930

Medical / Remediation Grade HEPA Vacuum

This is the closest to a conventional vacuum.

Mold particles are between 0.3 microns and 0.5 microns in size, so a vacuum which can trap particles of 0.3 microns in size is needed. A vacuum where the HEPA filters meet the EN1822 standard is the minimum recommended.

The Nilfisk Industrial models are high performing vacuums used in medical facilities and by mold remediation companies.

Lower Standards

Conventional Consumer Grade Vacuum

I do not recommend consumer grade vacuums, particularly where people have Asthma or other respiratory complaints.
The primary goal of these vacuums is typically not to clean to the highest standards.

Many of the consumer oriented design features can move air quality further down the list. Easy open latches, retractable cords, tool storage compartments, lightweight motors, quiet motors and blockage access panels. Features which require additional rubber gaskets are not good because this introduces additional points of potential air leakage.

The additional complexity of these parts makes cleaning and servicing the vacuum itself more difficult.
This also makes it more difficult to notice when something fails.
